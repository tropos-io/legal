# **ANNEX J \- INFORMATION SECURITY FRAMEWORK**

**J.1	Scope.** This Annex applies to all engagements unless a Statement of Work expressly excludes it. Where the Enterprise Addendum applies, Clause E.9 supplements this Annex; in the event of conflict, Clause E.9 prevails. Security measures shall be proportionate to: (a) the nature and sensitivity of Client Data processed; (b) the criticality of the Services; and (c) the state of the art and cost of implementation.

**J.2	Security Management.** The Supplier shall maintain an Information Security Management System ("ISMS") based on ISO/IEC 27001, the NIST Cybersecurity Framework or equivalent recognised standard. The ISMS shall be reviewed annually. The Supplier shall designate a security-responsible role with authority to enforce security policies in connection with the Services.

**J.3	Risk Assessment.** The Supplier shall conduct an annual risk assessment covering assets, threats, vulnerabilities and controls relevant to the Services and Client Data. Risk treatment shall follow a documented process. Residual risks exceeding the Supplier's risk appetite shall be reported to the Client on request.

**J.4	Access Controls.** The Supplier shall implement role-based access controls on a need-to-know, least-privilege basis. Access shall be removed within five (5) Business Days of personnel departure or role change. Privileged access shall require: (a) individual accountability; (b) multi-factor authentication; and (c) quarterly review.

**J.5	Encryption.** Client Data shall be encrypted in transit (TLS 1.2 or higher) and at rest (AES-256 or equivalent). Encryption keys shall be managed with separation of duties between custodians and users.

**J.6	Vulnerability Management.** The Supplier shall maintain a vulnerability management programme including automated scanning, classification by CVSS or equivalent and risk-proportionate remediation. Indicative remediation targets: Critical (CVSS 9.0+) \- forty-eight (48) hours; High (7.0–8.9) \- seven (7) calendar days; Medium (4.0–6.9) \- thirty (30) calendar days.

**J.7	Incident Response.** The Supplier shall maintain an incident response plan covering identification, containment, eradication, recovery and lessons learned.

On identifying a Security Incident affecting Client Data or the Services, the Supplier shall: (a) notify the Client within seventy-two (72) hours; (b) provide an initial assessment (nature, scope, data affected, containment measures, expected timeline); (c) provide updates at least every twenty-four (24) hours during active investigation; and (d) deliver a written root-cause analysis within thirty (30) calendar days of resolution.

This Clause J.7 is without prejudice to notification obligations under the Data Processing Addendum (Annex D).

**J.8	Personnel Security.** Personnel with access to Client Data or Client systems shall be subject to: (a) confidentiality obligations no less protective than Clause 5 of the MSA; and (b) security awareness training at commencement and annually thereafter. Enhanced vetting (including background checks) applies where specified in a Statement of Work, subject to applicable law.

**J.9	Supply-Chain Security.** Where subcontractors process Client Data or access Client systems, the Supplier shall: (a) impose security obligations no less protective than this Annex; and (b) conduct reasonable due diligence before engagement and periodically thereafter. The Supplier shall maintain a register of such subcontractors, available to the Client on request. Supplier liability for subcontractors is governed by Clause 2.8 of the MSA.

**J.10	Physical Security.** Where the Supplier processes Client Data in facilities under its control (excluding third-party cloud infrastructure, co-working spaces and remote environments), it shall maintain physical access controls and environmental protections proportionate to data classification. For co-working and remote environments, endpoint security under Clause J.12 applies.

**J.11	Network Security.** The Supplier shall: (a) segment networks used for the Services from general corporate networks where feasible; (b) maintain firewall, intrusion detection and logging capabilities proportionate to the Services; and (c) retain logs relevant to Client Data processing for ninety (90) calendar days.

**J.12	Endpoint Security.** Devices used to access Client Data or Client systems shall have: (a) full-disk encryption; (b) current anti-malware protection; (c) automated patching; and (d) remote-wipe capability for mobile devices. Personal devices may access Client Data only where managed under MDM or equivalent controls.

**J.13	Secure Development.** Where the Services include software development, the Supplier shall maintain: (a) code review procedures; (b) static or dynamic application security testing before production deployment; (c) dependency scanning for third-party libraries; and (d) separation of development, testing and production environments.

**J.14	Audit and Assurance.** On reasonable request (not more than once per twelve (12) month period), the Supplier shall provide: (a) evidence of current security certifications (ISO 27001, SOC 2 Type II or equivalent); or (b) a completed security questionnaire in standard format (SIG, CAIQ or equivalent). Audits beyond the foregoing require agreement on scope, timing and cost under a Statement of Work or Change Order. Statutory audit rights under Clause 11.11 of the MSA are unaffected.

**J.15	Continuous Improvement.** The Supplier shall monitor the threat landscape and update security measures as reasonably necessary. Material changes shall be notified to the Client on request.
